The Arctic Sea Ice
Approximately 30° South of the North Pole the Arctic Circle defines the extent of the Arctic, the northernmost region of Earth.
The Arctic sea ice is frozen ocean water that increases and decreases in extent with the seasons. Normally, during September it reaches its smallest extent while in March it reaches its largest extent.

However there has been a dramatic decline of the arctic sea ice extent in the past 30 years.
Specifically, September 2012 marked a record minimum Sea ice melting contributes to the acceleration of global warming since the large ice sheets are replaced by the dark ocean that absorbes more sunlight which further heats up the water.
In April 2022 the NCIDC recorded 14.06 million km2 of sea ice, which is 630,000 km2 below 1981-2010 average
